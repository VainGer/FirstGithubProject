Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone is a British-American fantasy film based on the first novel by J. K. Rowling, released on 16 November 2001. It is directed by Chris Columbus, written by Steve Kloves, and produced by David Heyman. The film stars Daniel Radcliffe, Rupert Grint, Emma Watson, John Cleese, Robbie Coltrane, Richard Griffiths, Richard Harris, Ian Hart, John Hurt, Alan Rickman, Fiona Shaw, Maggie Smith, Sean Biggerstaff, Warwick Davis, Julie Walters, Zoë Wanamaker, David Bradley, and Tom Felton. For the American, Indian, and Philippine release, just as was done with the book, the film was given the alternate title Harry Potter and the Sorcerer's Stone. The film grossed $974 million worldwide in its initial release, making it the highest-grossing film of 2001 and the second highest-grossing film of all time at the time (behind Titanic).
